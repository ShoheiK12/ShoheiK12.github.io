My profile

This is my history.

1990
I was born in Hyogo and raised in Osaka, Japan.
2009
I graduated from high school in Osaka, Japan.
2013
I graduated from Doshisha University with a Bachelor of Law in Kyoto, Japan. My dream was to become a public prosecutor.
2015
I graduated from Ritsumeikan University with a Juris Doctor in Kyoto, Japan. Although I tried a bar exam to become a public prosecutor, I failed to pass it.
2019
I moved to Australia on a working holiday because I wanted to tackle new challenges. Until 2024, I worked across Australia, such as in Western Australia, Tasmania, Queensland, Victoria and New South Wales.
2024
I enrolled in a Master of Computer Science at the University of Wollongong, Australia, to become a software engineer. Despite studying law at a university in Japan, my decision to pursue computer science at an Australian university stems from the realization that, as a software engineer, I can have a more profound impact on the Japanese judicial system than by engaging in legal practice. In Japan, many people cannot obtain information about legal remedies in the first place or access the remedy system easily due to the slow integration of IT into the Japanese judicial system. Therefore, as a software engineer, I believe that by further promoting the introduction of IT in this system, I can create a foundation for judicial relief by increasing the number of people who can access the judicial system, which will in turn help those who need legal relief in the future.
2025
I have graduated from the University of Wollongong with a Master of Computer Science, specialising in software engineering, with distinction.
